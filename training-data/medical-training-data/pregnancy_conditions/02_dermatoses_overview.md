## OVERVIEW
Dermatoses of pregnancy are a heterogeneous, distinct group of skin disorders specific to pregnancy and the postpartum period. They arise from interaction of hormonal, immunological, and mechanical factors. These conditions range from benign and transient to severe and chronic.

### Three Main Factors Causing Skin Changes in Pregnancy
1. Increase in hormone levels
2. Intravascular volume expansion
3. Skin compression from the pregnant uterus

### Common (Normal) Skin Changes in Pregnancy
- Melasma
- Venous congestion, varicosities
- Spider telangiectasias
- Palmar erythema
- Cutis marmorata
- Acrochordons (skin tags)
- Striae gravidarum

## THE 5 SPECIFIC DERMATOSES OF PREGNANCY

### 1. PEMPHIGOID GESTATIONIS (PG)
*(See dedicated file: 01_pemphigoid_gestationis.md)*
- Incidence: 1 in 50,000–60,000 pregnancies
- Autoimmune bullous disease; autoantibodies against BP180 and BP230
- Begins in placenta → antibodies cross-react with skin
- Characterized by: eosinophilic spongiosis, eosinophilic epitheliotropism, dense superficial dermal eosinophils
- Positive DIF (C3 linear deposits at BMZ) is diagnostic
- Most significant risk factor: previous PG

### 2. POLYMORPHIC ERUPTION OF PREGNANCY (PEP) / PUPPP
*(See dedicated file: 03_PUPPP_PEP.md)*
- Incidence: 1 in 200 pregnancies — MOST COMMON gestational dermatosis
- Typically third trimester, primigravidas
- More common when fetus is male; 75% primiparous
- Begins in abdominal striae (spares umbilicus — key differential from PG)
- Negative DIF
- Benign — no fetal morbidity

### 3. INTRAHEPATIC CHOLESTASIS OF PREGNANCY (ICP)
*(See dedicated file: 04_intrahepatic_cholestasis.md)*
- Incidence varies geographically: 0.32% (white US) to 28% (Araucanian Indian)
- Europe: ≤1.5%
- US Hispanic: up to 5.6%
- Pruritus + elevated bile acids ± elevated liver enzymes
- Significant fetal risks: preterm labor, stillbirth

### 4. PUSTULAR PSORIASIS OF PREGNANCY (PPP) / IMPETIGO HERPETIFORMIS
- Very rare dermatosis
- Most common in third trimester, often primigravidas
- May be caused by: hypocalcemia, low vitamin D, hyperparathyroidism, genetic variation, hormonal changes
- Poorly understood pathophysiology
- Appearance: sterile pustular eruption at flexural areas

### 5. ATOPIC ERUPTION OF PREGNANCY (AEP)
- MOST COMMON skin disorder in pregnancy (accounts for ~50% of all gestational dermatoses)
- Most cases present in **first trimester** (75% before third trimester — earlier than other dermatoses)
- Includes:
  - Eczema in pregnancy
  - Prurigo of pregnancy
  - Pruritic folliculitis of pregnancy
- Lesions:
  - **Eczema subtype:** primarily flexural or atopic sites
  - **Prurigo subtype:** excoriated papules on limbs/trunk
  - **Pruritic folliculitis:** erythematous follicular papules (second/third trimester), resembling steroid-induced acne
- Pathophysiology: exaggerated shift in immune response (normally occurs to prevent fetal rejection)

## COMPARISON TABLE: KEY DIFFERENTIATING FEATURES

| Feature | PG | PEP/PUPPP | ICP | AEP | PPP |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Incidence | 1/50,000 | 1/200 | 0.3–28% | ~50% of all | Very rare |
| Trimester onset | 2nd/3rd | 3rd | 2nd/3rd | 1st (usually) | 3rd |
| Primary symptom | Pruritus + bullae | Urticarial plaques | Pruritus (no rash initially) | Eczema/papules | Pustules |
| Umbilical involvement | Yes (90%) | No (spares umbilicus) | No | No | No |
| Face involvement | Spared | Spared | Spared | Can involve | Can involve |
| DIF | Positive (C3 linear) | Negative | Negative | Negative | Negative |
| Fetal risk | Yes (IUGR, preterm) | None | Yes (stillbirth, preterm) | Minimal | Yes |
| Recurrence | High (30-50%) | Rare | High | Variable | Yes |
| HLA association | DR3, DR4 | None strong | ABCB4 mutation | Atopy | None specific |
| Treatment | Corticosteroids, IVIG | Emollients, antihistamines, topical CS | UDCA, delivery at 38wk | Emollients, antihistamines | Corticosteroids, delivery |

## DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

### When to suspect a specific dermatosis:
1. **PG:** Periumbilical blisters/urticaria in 2nd/3rd trimester → DIF
2. **PEP:** Third trimester, primigravid, urticarial papules starting in striae → clinical diagnosis + negative DIF
3. **ICP:** Severe pruritus especially palms/soles at night, no primary rash → bile acids, LFTs
4. **AEP:** Eczema/prurigo early in pregnancy, atopic history → clinical
5. **PPP:** Pustular eruption at flexures, 3rd trimester → biopsy, Ca/Vit D levels
