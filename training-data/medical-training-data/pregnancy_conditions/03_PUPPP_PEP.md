# Polymorphic Eruption of Pregnancy (PEP) / PUPPP
## Source: StatPearls / NCBI Bookshelf (NIH) — Last Updated July 31, 2023
## Authors: Chourouk Chouk, Noureddine Litaiem
## URL: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK539700/

---

## NAMES & TERMINOLOGY
- **PEP** = Polymorphic Eruption of Pregnancy (preferred international term)
- **PUPPP** = Pruritic Urticarial Papules and Plaques of Pregnancy (used in the United States)
- Both names refer to the same condition

---

## OVERVIEW
- **Most common** gestational dermatosis
- Incidence: **1 in 200 pregnancies**
- Benign, pruritic, inflammatory skin disorder
- Polymorphous clinical presentation
- Normal laboratory tests
- **Negative direct immunofluorescence** (key differential from PG)

---

## EPIDEMIOLOGY
- Typically affects **primigravidas** in the **third trimester**
- Less frequent in immediate postpartum period
- 75% of affected women are primiparous
- More common when the fetus is male
- No tendency to recur in subsequent pregnancies (exception: may be less severe if it recurs in multiple-gestation pregnancy)
- No ethnic predisposition

---

## PATHOPHYSIOLOGY
- Not fully understood
- Possible mechanisms:
  - Abdominal distension causing damage to connective tissue → inflammatory response
  - Fetal cells (fetal DNA) may trigger maternal immune response
  - Male fetal DNA found in lesional skin of affected women → possible microchimerism trigger
- **No autoimmune basis** (unlike PG)

---

## HISTOPATHOLOGY
- **Early lesions:**
  - Epidermal and upper dermal edema
  - Focal mild spongiosis
  - Deeper dermal lymphohistiocytic perivascular infiltrate
  - May resemble arthropod bite reactions
  - T-helper lymphocytes predominate + variable eosinophils and neutrophils
- **Resolving stage:**
  - Acanthosis
  - Hyperkeratosis and parakeratosis
- DIF: usually negative; minimal nonspecific granular IgM, C3, IgA may be present (nonspecific)
- Indirect immunofluorescence for circulating IgG antibodies: negative

---

## CLINICAL FEATURES

### Onset
- Third trimester (rarely postpartum)
- Average onset: last 3–4 weeks of pregnancy

### Primary lesion site
- **Begins in abdominal striae** — critical differentiating feature
- **Umbilicus is SPARED** (contrast: PG starts periumbilically)

### Lesion types (polymorphic — hence the name)
- Urticarial papules and plaques (most common)
- Vesicles (rarely bullae)
- Target-like (erythema multiforme-like) lesions
- Eczematous lesions

### Spread
- Spreads from striae → abdomen → buttocks, thighs, arms
- Typically does NOT spread to face, palms, soles

### Symptom severity
- Pruritus can be intense

---

## DIAGNOSIS
- Clinical diagnosis — no specific lab test confirms PUPPP
- Key: normal laboratory values, negative DIF
- Perform DIF to exclude PG when in doubt

---

## DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
### Most important:
- **Pemphigoid Gestationis (PG):** overlap possible clinically; key differentials:
  - PG: umbilical involvement, positive DIF (C3 linear), earlier onset possible, more severe
  - PUPPP: negative DIF, spares umbilicus, third trimester primigravid
- **Atopic eczema:** personal/family atopy history, flexural distribution
- Drug eruptions
- Urticaria
- Viral exanthems

---

## TREATMENT
- **Topical corticosteroids** (moderate-to-high potency) — mainstay
- **Oral antihistamines**
- **General measures:**
  - Cool, soothing baths
  - Frequent application of emollients
  - Light cotton clothing
- **Severe/intractable cases:** systemic corticosteroids (short course)
- Early delivery: rarely used, only for intractable pruritus
- Alternative (case report): intramuscular autologous whole blood injection (postpartum PUPPP)

---

## PROGNOSIS
- Excellent maternal prognosis — no increased maternal risk
- **No fetal morbidities** — neonatal skin is usually not affected
- Self-limiting; resolves after delivery
- Recurrence in subsequent pregnancies unusual (except multiple-gestation pregnancies)

---

## INTERPROFESSIONAL APPROACH
- Close cooperation between:
  - Dermatologist
  - Obstetrician
  - Labor and delivery nurse
  - Neonatal pediatrician (for severe cases)
- Patients should understand PUPPP is well-recognized, limited in duration, implies no increased maternal/fetal risk
