## SYNONYMS
- Obstetric cholestasis (OC)
- Pruritus gravidarum
- Icterus gravidarum
- Recurrent jaundice of pregnancy
- Idiopathic jaundice of pregnancy

---

## OVERVIEW
ICP is the **most common liver disorder in pregnancy** and the most common hepatic disorder related to pregnancy. It is a reversible cholestatic disease of the late second and early third trimester. Characterized by pruritus, elevated serum bile acids, and abnormal liver function tests. Symptoms and biochemical abnormalities rapidly resolve after delivery.

## ETIOLOGY & PATHOGENESIS
- **Not fully understood — multifactorial**
- Key factors:
  1. **Hormonal:** elevated estrogen → cholestasis; high estrogen inhibits bile acid transport
  2. **Genetic susceptibility:** mutations in hepatobiliary transport proteins:
     - **MDR3 (ABCB4)** — at least 10 different mutations; ABCB4 variant associated with severe ICP
     - **BSEP (Bile Salt Export Pump)**
  3. **Environmental factors**
  4. Hormonal changes alter transport of sex steroids across canalicular and hepatic membranes
- High-dose oral contraceptives (high estrogen state) can similarly cause cholestasis
- Geographic variability and seasonal variation suggest environmental component

## CLINICAL PRESENTATION

### Onset
- Typically **late second trimester or early third trimester**
- Can start as early as second or third month (first trimester cases rare but documented)

### Symptoms
- **Pruritus** — hallmark symptom; often worse at night, especially palms and soles
- Pruritus without a primary rash (no blisters, no urticaria)
- Jaundice in some cases (mild)
- May have dark urine, pale stools if jaundice develops
- Fatigue

### What ICP does NOT present with
- No primary skin rash (unlike PG or PUPPP)
- No bullae or vesicles

---

## LABORATORY FINDINGS

### Key findings:
- **Elevated total serum bile acids** (most sensitive marker) — may be ONLY abnormality in some cases
- **Elevated ALT** (rarely >250 IU/L)
- **Elevated AST**
- Alkaline phosphatase may be elevated (interpret carefully — normally elevated in pregnancy due to placental production)
- Elevated bilirubin (if jaundice present)

### Critical threshold:
- Serum bile acids **>40 μmol/L** = SEVERE ICP → significantly increased risk of fetal complications
- Fetal complications correlate with bile acid level; screening throughout pregnancy recommended

---

## FETAL RISKS
| Complication | Rate |
|---|---|
| Preterm delivery | 19–60% |
| Meconium staining of amniotic fluid | 27% |
| Fetal bradycardia | 14% |
| Fetal distress | 22–41% |
| Fetal loss/stillbirth | 0.4–4.1% |

- Risks particularly increased when fasting serum bile acids >40 μmol/L
- Mechanism of fetal harm: not completely elucidated; bile acids may cause cardiac arrhythmia in fetal cardiomyocytes

---

## TREATMENT

### First-Line
- **Ursodeoxycholic acid (UDCA) 10–20 mg/kg/day**
  - Modifies bile acid pool
  - Relieves pruritus
  - Improves liver biochemical tests
  - May improve fetal outcomes
  - Note: Some debate persists about effect on perinatal outcomes (ongoing investigation)

### Second-Line (if UDCA fails)
- **Cholestyramine** (bile acid sequestrant) — less effective than UDCA
- **Hydroxyzine** — symptomatic relief of pruritus

### Investigational Therapies (as of 2024–2025)
- **Volixibat** (IBAT inhibitor)
- **FXR agonists**
- **4-phenylbutyrate**
- **NorUDCA**

### Delivery
- **Recommend delivery at 38 weeks gestation** (or 36 weeks if severe cholestasis)
- Lung maturity should be established before 38-week delivery
- **Vitamin K** should be given ≥6 hours before delivery to minimize postpartum hemorrhage risk (ICP can impair fat-soluble vitamin absorption)

---

## FOLLOW-UP
- Symptoms resolve within **2–3 weeks after delivery**
- If pruritus and ALP elevation do NOT resolve postpartum → consider alternate conditions:
  - Primary biliary cholangitis
  - Familial cholestatic disorder (MDR3 mutations)

---

## RECURRENCE RISK
- High risk of ICP recurring in subsequent pregnancies
- Also associated with increased risk of biliary system diseases later in life

---

## DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
- Pemphigoid gestationis (PG) — has primary skin lesions; ICP does not
- PUPPP — has primary rash; ICP does not
- Acute fatty liver of pregnancy (AFLP) — more severe hepatic dysfunction
- HELLP syndrome — accompanied by hypertension, hemolysis, thrombocytopenia
- Viral hepatitis
- Cholecystitis / cholelithiasis
- Primary biliary cholangitis
